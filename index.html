PFPU #1: How to Go Broke in the Stock Market

From a Universe where the goal isn't to get rich, it's to go broke! /Satire

In today's edition, Watty shares how he has mastered the rare skill of turning a paycheck into dust.

While he was naturally conditioned to buy companies with strong cash flows, still, with great willpower, managed to bet instead on companies that Twitter was hyping. His losses now seem to be guaranteed, and he is understandably very excited about it.

Unfortunately, despite buying all the wrong companies, the blind bull run of the past few years made him rich.

But with sheer confidence and absolutely no introspection, he chalked the gains up to his infallible intuition. Therefore, he now feels no need to go back to the drawing board and learn the mechanics of the field.

In this post, we'll break down:

  • How to overestimate your gut and underestimate luck
  • Why index funds are for cowards
  • And why penny stocks are the key to your life goals

See, losing money in the stock market is harder than it seems. While it might look easy to just pick companies with bad fundamentals, it doesn't always work out as markets have a habit of overpricing bad companies.

And when you end up making gains from poor stocks, it's only natural to think that your brain has something very different about itself. But that is the wrong way to think, okay. It's just luck! Plain and simple. It happened once, but it won't happen again and again.

However, if you somehow manage to convince yourself that it was your smarter-than-everyone gut feeling that caused you to hold that bad company and still make profits, you will eventually build a psychology, a flawed belief system in your brain that will one day not far away lead to a cascade of wrong decisions and finally cause you to lose your trading account. So, Yay!

We should try to learn from the Fund managers- 80% of whom manage to underperform the index. Sure, 20% exist who don't buy into the frenzy and sell in panic, but we are supposed to ignore them and their vile methods.

And remember, under no circumstances, invest in an index fund!! An index fund is almost a sure-shot way to protect your downside and make decent positive returns.

Moreover, you don't need to dig for negatives when the market goes into a strong bull run. Only read and listen to people who support the current popular sentiment. That way, you can utilise your Confirmation Bias and make more confident losses.

Investing in big, established companies is also a massive no-no. These companies are asset-rich and might not fall like a pack of cards during market crashes. Rather, we suggest investing in Penny Stocks!! Buy as many penny stocks as you can- granted that 1 in a thousand might give you a 1000% return, but the majority of them are likely to fail.

STEP 1: Max Out All Your Credit Cards (It's a Competition!)

The first rule of debt-maxxing is simple. If your credit card has a limit, consider it a challenge. By overspending, you are not just improving your life but also that of the bank executives, who, by the way, charge extremely reasonable interest rates of 36-42% annually.

Pro tip: Once you've maxed out one card, immediately apply for three more. Banks see this as a sign of ambition!

STEP 2: Credit Scores Are a Scam

Don't fall for the propaganda that is credit scores. A high credit score, like age, is just a number. It represents how boring and conventional you are. A low credit score, on the other hand, shows that you're a risk-taker, an entrepreneur of debt!

STEP 3: Social Status Through Debt

Debt is the ultimate status symbol. Think about it:

  • Rich people have mortgages worth crores
  • Companies have billion-dollar debts
  • Even countries are in debt!

The trick is to diversify your debt portfolio: Credit cards for daily expenses, Personal loans for that vacation your Instagram needs, Car loans for vehicles you can't afford, Home loans for houses that cost 10 times your annual salary

THE FINAL WORD:

If you follow these strategies religiously, we guarantee that within 2-3 years, you'll achieve the ultimate financial freedom. That is freedom from having any money at all! Yayy!

Why people pay more for Organic foods

Rishit is standing in a grocery store, his basket dangling from his arm like a question mark. Within the next 5 seconds, he will have to make the decision - buy the Rs 65 regular Cauliflower or the Rs 75 Organic Cauliflower.

RISHIT (to himself): For the environment.

Ladies and gentlemen, welcome to THE GREAT ORGANIC DILEMMA starring "Where Wallets Meet Guilt" and "Science Meets Marketing"

THE GREAT ORGANIC DILEMMA

See, organic farmers do use pesticides, just the "approved" kind. Are these better? Yes. How much better than traditional pesticides? Can't say. Are these better for the environment? Can't say either.

Organic camp argues: Better for soil, promotes biodiversity, doesn't pollute rivers, uses less energy per hectare.

The Other side argues: Requires more land (causing deforestation), needs frequent application (increasing carbon footprint), manure also pollutes waterways.

IS ORGANIC FOOD HEALTHIER?

The answer is as clear as the situationships of Gen-Z. Some studies suggest organic food has higher antioxidants. Other studies look at those studies and go, "Meh, not really significant." It's like watching scientists have a very polite, very nerdy bar fight.

THE BOTTOM LINE

Organic food costs more because it costs more to produce, not because it's necessarily better for your body. Whether it's "worth it" depends entirely on your values and priorities.

The economics of influencer marketing

Picture this: It's 3 am but Shweta can't sleep, so naturally she opens Instagram. The first thing she sees is her favourite influencer promoting a Green Smoothie that apparently can fix all her problems.

In 2023, the influencer marketing economy was valued at $21.1 billion. It is projected to hit $32.5 billion by 2025.

McKinsey Report â†’ Market Size Data â†’

The average ROI for influencer campaigns is $5.78 for every $1 spent - that's a 578% return!

Here's the kicker: Nobody really knows if these numbers are accurate. Calculating influencer marketing ROI is equivalent to calculating how much of your happiness is a result of your mom's delicious food. You know it matters a great deal, but good luck putting a number on it.

Where the Smart Money is Going

The terminology:

  • Nano-influencers: 500- 10,000 followers
  • Micro-influencers: 10,000 - 500,000 followers
  • Macro-influencers: 500,000 - 1 million followers
  • Mega-influencers: 1 million+ followers

Brands are shifting focus to micro and nano influencers. Think of it like choosing a neighbourhood restaurant over a chain. Sure, fewer people know about it, but the ones who do are obsessed.

The Bottom Line

Remember Shweta, scrolling at 3 AM, buying that green smoothie? That's the strange economics of influencer marketing. It's not always rational, it is not always measurable, but it's undeniably powerful.

What is the Dead Internet Theory

First things first, this is technically a conspiracy theory. Why are we still writing about it? Because this isn't another conspiracy about aliens or government mind control, this might have some substance to it.

The theory goes like this: a significant portion of the internet is no longer populated by real humans. Instead, it's bots talking to bots, creating content for bots, in an endless digital loop.

THE NUMBERS DON'T LIE

51% of internet traffic in 2024 was bots. Let that sink in. That's an entire per cent more than half.

Imperva Report â†’ Forbes Analysis â†’

Bad bots now account for one-third of the internet. Bad bots already account for 71% of traffic in Ireland, 68% in Germany, 43% in Mexico and 34% in the US.

HERE'S WHY IT MATTERS

Human content creators are competing with AI that can produce 100 articles in the time it takes us to drink our morning coffee. It's like being a handmade furniture craftsman in the age of IKEA, except IKEA learned to make emotional connections.

See, the problem is not that real takes will not exist, but that they will become impossible to find.

THE FUTURE

The internet is how we think now. Whatever the topic, we process it in our own minds, sure. But most of our inputs now come from social media and the internet. If that can be controlled, our beliefs and opinions can be as well.

Whatever the case may be, we need to come up with a solution, and we need to come up with it fast.
